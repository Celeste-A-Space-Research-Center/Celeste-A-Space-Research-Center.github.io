Welcome to CELESTE: A Space Research Center

(Where circuits hum in cosmic harmony, and algorithms dance among the stars.)

G. Narayanamma Institute of Technology and Science (GNITS) stands as a distinguished research institution dedicated to advancing women's engineering education and pushing the boundaries of scientific exploration. Founded in 1997 by the late Sri G. Pulla Reddy, GNITS holds the distinction of being the second women's engineering institute established in India, with a rich legacy of academic excellence and innovation. GNITS was established in Shaikpet, Hyderabad.

Celeste: A Space Research Centre, officially inaugurated on February 23rd, 2024, under the auspices of esteemed dignitaries Dr. K. Ramesh Reddy, Principal of GNITS, and Dr. M. Seetha, Dean of Research and Development, marks a significant step in GNITS's commitment to advancing space exploration. This momentous occasion coincided with the historic revelation at the University of Southampton concerning the measurement of gravity at a microscopic level, symbolizing the dawn of a new era in space exploration at GNITS. Operated by diligent student researchers under the guidance of esteemed faculty members Dr. Swapna Raghunath and Dr. Rajesh T, Celeste is dedicated to leveraging principles of computer science and electronics to propel advancements in astrophysics and space science.

Inspired by the remarkable achievements of our alumni, GNITS students are imbued with a profound sense of purpose and determination. They are driven not only by the desire to follow in the footsteps of these esteemed individuals but also by a shared vision of pushing the boundaries of knowledge and discovery. Motivated by the groundbreaking contributions of former graduates such as Ms. Bireddy Ramya, Scientist at the Physical Research Laboratory, ISRO, and Ms. Sai Veena, Scientist at the UR Rao Satellite Center, ISRO, who played pivotal roles in the success of the Chandrayaan 3 mission, GNITS students are propelled towards excellence in their pursuit of space research.
With access to state-of-the-art facilities and guidance from distinguished faculty members, GNITS students are equipped to embark on ambitious projects at Celeste: A Space Research Centre. Utilizing cutting-edge computational methodologies, they delve into the complexities of astrophysics and space science, leveraging their expertise in computer science and electronics. Their research endeavors aim not only to unravel the mysteries of the cosmos but also to make tangible contributions to disciplines such as metrology and astronomy.
